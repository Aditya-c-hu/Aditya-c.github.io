Using Floyd-Warshall Algorithm for optimal pump placement for most efficiency:

Floyd-Warshall helps identify the longest shortest path (i.e., the path that takes the longest time for water to reach). Placing a pump near nodes on this path reduces travel time and avoids water pressure drops. By placing pumps at optimal locations, the total pipeline length, operational costs, and energy usage are minimized. The algorithm helps find paths that reduce overall water transportation costs.

Shortest path During Maintainance:

Dijkstra’s algorithm is ideal for finding the shortest path from a single source to all other nodes in a graph. This makes it useful for determining the optimal route to divert water flow during pipeline maintenance.The algorithm can adapt to real-time changes in the network, ensuring uninterrupted water supply.

Spanning tree of Water flow :

Kruskal’s algorithm is useful for designing and optimizing water pipeline networks by constructing a Minimum Spanning Tree (MST). This ensures that all junctions in the pipeline system are connected with the minimum total cost or least pipe length.Easily adapts to new pipeline connections or expansion projects.
